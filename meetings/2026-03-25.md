## Discussion Topics

### I. External Messages and IETF Landscape Review

Song summarized several recent external messages relevant to the Community Group, including:

- A message from **Ben** about the **Conduit** protocol/product
- A message discussing **ATEP, AVIS, VCAP, AP2, bonding**, and related specifications
- A message from **Paula** regarding **AI KRCG**

Song also reviewed an IETF-oriented classification diagram of current agent-related work. The diagram grouped ongoing work into three broad domains:

- **Protocol interoperability**
- **Trust and security**
- **Vertical applications**

The group agreed that this framing is useful for understanding where different efforts overlap and where coordination gaps are emerging.

### II. Fragmentation in AI Agent Authentication and Delegation

George explained that fragmentation is becoming a major challenge across the AI agent ecosystem. Multiple organizations, including W3C, IETF, and other foundations, are exploring similar authentication, delegation, and authorization problems, but often from different assumptions and with different priorities.

A key concern raised during the discussion was that overlapping proposals are being developed in parallel without enough coordination, especially inside and around the IETF OAuth ecosystem. The group noted that business pressure to launch practical solutions quickly is encouraging teams to optimize for immediate use cases rather than convergence on shared standards.

The discussion highlighted the risk that several partially compatible ecosystems may emerge, making long-term interoperability harder to achieve.

### III. Outreach and Coordination Strategy

Victor emphasized the need for stronger outreach to related organizations such as the **Linux Foundation**, **IIW**, and relevant **IETF** efforts. He noted that no single organization is likely to be accepted as the sole decision-maker, so broader communication is necessary to reduce duplication and encourage convergence.

Song agreed that cross-community communication is important and noted that W3C should also improve internal coordination across different Community Groups and Working Groups that are addressing adjacent topics. He planned to raise this coordination and oversight question at the upcoming **W3C AC meeting in April 2026**.

The group also agreed on the following outreach actions:

- Invite **Ben** to demonstrate or present **Conduit** at an upcoming CG meeting
- Invite the contributor discussing **ATEP, AVIS, VCAP, AP2, bonding**, and related efforts to explain those specifications and associated organizations in a future session
- Review **Paula's AI KRCG** message and determine whether ongoing communication or collaboration would be valuable

George also mentioned upcoming participation in **IIW** and the related AI agent session in April, which could provide a useful opportunity for outreach on behalf of the group.

### IV. DID Compatibility, Messaging, and wiki.AI Discussion

George asked how different DID methods could remain compatible and extensible across different entities and application environments. Sean explained that their approach is similar to a federated messaging system such as Matrix: multiple servers can interoperate as long as they follow the same communication protocol and message format.

Sean and Gaowei introduced the **wiki.AI** approach to agent communication, emphasizing that it is designed to support communication in both **on-chain** and **off-chain** environments through **self-managed DIDs**. They noted that the architecture can be extended to support different DID methods and may serve as a bridge between different DID ecosystems.

The group also discussed whether an IAM-related service object could be defined within DID methods to simplify service integration. Sean suggested that existing instant messaging protocols might also be adapted to support DID-based communication, with the communication method negotiated between agents when needed.

The participants agreed that additional demonstrations would be helpful to test real communication scenarios, including AI-native messaging approaches and integrations with existing Web2 services.

### V. Community Questions and Scope Clarification

A new participant, **Jack**, asked about the difference between **ANP** and the **AI Agent Protocol** work discussed in this group. Sean clarified that the current discussion is focused on enabling agent communication in both on-chain and off-chain environments, with particular attention to interoperability, identity, and communication mechanisms rather than a single product-specific implementation.

The discussion reinforced the group's current role as a coordination and exploration venue for emerging agent protocol work rather than a finalized standards body for a single protocol stack.
