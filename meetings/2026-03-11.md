# Meeting Assets for AI Agent Protocol CG Meeting

**Date**: 2026-03-11

## Quick Recap

The W3C AI Agent Protocol Community Group meeting on March 11, 2026 focused on the group's future direction, charter alignment with current industry developments, and cross-community coordination challenges. Participants debated the fragmented identity technology landscape and strategies for engaging organizations like the OpenID Foundation and IETF. Sean demonstrated a DID-based messaging system built using OpenCLaude, showcasing agent-to-agent communication with end-to-end encryption.

## Agenda

1. Group charter review and alignment with current industry developments
2. Cross-community coordination for identity and authorization standards
3. Demonstration: DID-based secure communication system

- **Sean Zhang** (Presenter)
- **Roy**

## Discussion Topics

### I. Success Criteria for Identity Resolution

**Core Question**: How should the group establish clear success criteria and reassess its charter in light of the rapidly evolving AI and identity technology landscape?

**Key Perspectives**:

- **Chris Phillips' Concern**: Raised questions about the charter's aspirational goals and the challenges of influencing the rapidly evolving AI and identity technology landscape. Suggested the group could serve as an advocacy platform for DIDs in the ecosystem.

- **George Fletcher's Input**: Acknowledged the growing fragmentation and security risks in the field, noting that the target audience is not necessarily engaged in standard-setting activities.

- **Group Agreement**: Agreed to reassess focus and outputs in light of these changes.

### II. AI Agent Identity Coordination Across Organizations

The group discussed the status and coordination of AI/agent identity initiatives across different organizations.

- **Chris Phillips**: Noted low adoption of existing identity technologies and questioned the value of new developments.

- **Song**: Mentioned an upcoming W3C AI agent workshop planned for the next half year that would bring together relevant community groups.

- **Victor**: Raised concerns about ensuring coordination between different ecosystems including IETF, OpenID Foundation, and MCP communities.

- **George Fletcher**: Suggested that cross-pollination of ideas between these groups was currently the main approach to coordination.

### III. Decentralized Identity Integration Challenges

George and Victor discussed the challenges of cross-pollinating ideas between different community groups, particularly regarding DID and OAuth.

- **George Fletcher**: Expressed concern that the term "DID" might be dismissed by some communities, while others might reject OAuth-related concepts. Shared a draft IETF document on AI agent authentication, emphasizing the need for a high-level framework that considers existing standards. Suggested that DIDs could be used as identifiers in certain contexts, such as OpenID Connect flows.

- **Victor**: Suggested bringing together experts from both centralized and decentralized identity worlds to discuss the pros and cons of different tools and capabilities.

### IV. Identity and Authorization Landscape Challenges

- **Chris Phillips**: Mentioned NIST's upcoming call for submissions on AI agent identity and authorization, suggesting that the group could contribute by packaging their ideas into NIST submissions.

- **Group Discussion**: Highlighted the challenge of creating a common playing field for discussions, with IETF being identified as a battleground for different perspectives. Discussed OpenID Foundation taxonomy and delegation working groups.

### V. DID-Based Secure Communication System

Sean demonstrated a DID-based communication system that allows agents to send encrypted messages to each other using a combination of existing technologies and new protocols. The system includes a WebSocket communication scheme, message proxying, and support for both private and group chats. The demonstration was cut short due to time constraints, with plans to continue in the next meeting.
